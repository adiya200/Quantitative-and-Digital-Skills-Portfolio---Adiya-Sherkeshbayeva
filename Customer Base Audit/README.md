Project 2: Customer Base Audit (KazMarket)
Overview
This project involves a deep dive into customer transactional data to evaluate the "health" of an online retail business. Instead of looking at total sales, I analyzed individual customer behaviors to identify patterns of profitability and retention.
Quantitative Skills Demonstrated
Decile Analysis: Segmented customers by profit to identify value concentration.
Cohort Analysis: Tracked behavioral changes in groups over a 4-year period.
Multiplicative Decomposition: Analyzed revenue through the lens of active base size, frequency, and margin.
Retention Modeling: Calculated survival rates and identified the "loyal core" of the customer base.
Key Findings
Profit Concentration: The top 10% of customers generate 40% of total profit.
Retention: A sharp 88% churn occurs after the first purchase, but remaining customers show increasing order frequency over time.
Seasonality: Consistent Q4 spikes across all cohorts indicate high responsiveness to holiday promotions.
